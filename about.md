# About

I am QJC, and Data Sculptures is my studio practice built around machine-generated moving image works.

## How We Create Data Sculptures

Each sculpture begins with a curated visual dataset. I train a generative model to learn the material language of the archive, then explore latent space to find trajectories with emotional and formal tension.

I treat those trajectories like compositional paths. The model is guided through selected regions, frame by frame, to produce evolving forms that feel both algorithmic and painterly.

From these long-form outputs, I isolate specific time windows as collectible editions. Every clip is edited for rhythm, continuity, and visual pressure, then released as a numbered Data Sculpture.

## Why This Process

The goal is not automation for its own sake. The goal is to shape computation into a precise visual object: a work with structure, atmosphere, and authorship.
